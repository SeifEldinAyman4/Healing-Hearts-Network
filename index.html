Most Common Issues

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are actually serious and often fatal illnesses that are associated with severe disturbances in people's eating behaviors and related thoughts and emotions. Preoccupation with food, body weight, and shape may also signal an eating disorder.

Addictions

Addiction is a neuropsychological disorder characterized by a persistent and intense urge to use a drug or engage in a behavior that produces natural reward, despite substantial harm and other negative consequences. Repetitive drug use often alters brain function in ways that perpetuate craving, and weakens (but does not completely negate) self-control.

Body Image

Body dysmorphic disorder (BDD), or body dysmorphia, is a mental health condition where a person spends a lot of time worrying about flaws in their appearance. These flaws are often unnoticeable to others. People of any age can have BDD, but it's most common in teenagers and young adults. It affects both men and women.

Gender Differences in Social Media Platform Usage

This graph illustrates the number of people using various social media platforms, categorized by gender. Instagram and Twitter are the most popular platforms for both genders. However, women tend to be more active on Instagram, while men are more active on Twitter. Facebook, LinkedIn, and WhatsApp show relatively balanced usage between genders. Telegram and Snapchat have a slightly higher female user base.

Gender Differences in Dominant Emotions

This graph illustrates the prevalence of different dominant emotions among males and females. Both genders report happiness as the most common dominant emotion. However, women are more likely to experience sadness and anxiety, while men are more likely to experience anger and boredom. Neutral emotions are relatively balanced between the genders.

Relationship Between Dominant Emotion and Daily Usage Time

This graph illustrates the average daily usage time for individuals experiencing different dominant emotions. Happiness is associated with the highest average daily usage time, followed by anxiety and anger. Sadness, neutrality, and boredom exhibit lower average daily usage times.
